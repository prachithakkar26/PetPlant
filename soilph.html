Optimal Soil pH Levels for Various Plants

Most plants thrive in soil with a pH level between 6.0 and 7.012. This range ensures good nutrient availability for plant uptake. However, some plants have specific pH preferences:

  • Acid-loving plants (e.g., blueberries, azaleas):
    • pH 4.5-6.03.
  • Neutral to slightly alkaline plants (e.g., ferns, asparagus):
    • pH 6.0-7.53.
It’s important to test your soil’s pH and adjust it as needed to match the requirements of the plants you’re growing.
